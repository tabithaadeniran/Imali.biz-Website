Plain English First

What is
tokenisation?

"Imagine a plot of land worth RWF 375 million. Instead of one person buying it all, 5,000 people each own a digital certificate worth RWF 75,000. When the land appreciates or generates rental income — everyone earns proportionally."

A token is simply a digital certificate of ownership stored on the blockchain — an unforgeable, publicly verifiable record that says "this person owns X% of this asset." Think of it like a share in a company, except instead of a company, it's a physical piece of land or property in Rwanda.

The blockchain is the record-keeper. Just like the NLA (National Land Authority) keeps a register of who owns land, the blockchain keeps a digital register that cannot be changed, faked, or lost. Every token issued by Imali is tied to a real, verified, NLA-registered parcel.

When a project earns money — from rent, from sale, or from milestone completions — the smart contract automatically distributes your share to your wallet. No banks, no delays, no middlemen deciding when you get paid.

Step by Step

From land to token

Here's exactly how a piece of Rwanda's land becomes digital equity — and how you earn from it.

01
🏡
Land is Listed & Verified

A landowner submits their parcel on The Land Bank. Imali verifies title deeds, confirms UPI with the NLA, and runs the Readiness Assessment.

02
📋
Legal Wrap Applied

Our Compliance Hub creates a legal entity (SPV) around the land. This is what investors are actually buying shares of — with full title protection.

03
⛓️
Smart Contract Deployed

A smart contract on Hedera Hashgraph is created. It defines how many tokens exist, who can own them, and exactly when and how earnings are distributed.

04
💰
Tokens Issued to Investors

Investors commit funds via escrow. Tokens are issued proportionally to their wallets. Each token = a verified, legal share of the asset.

05
📈
You Earn as It Grows

At every project milestone — groundbreaking, completion, rental commencement, or sale — the smart contract releases returns directly to your wallet.

Common Questions

Things people
wonder about

🔐
Is my ownership legally real?

Yes. Every token is backed by a legal SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) that holds the title deed. Your token is a legal equity share, not just a digital receipt. The Compliance Hub handles all legal documentation.

💸
Can I sell my tokens before the project ends?

The secondary market is coming. Currently, tokens are held for the duration of the project hold period. Early exit requests are reviewed case-by-case. This protects all co-investors from sudden liquidity events.

🌍
What blockchain does Imali use?

We use Hedera Hashgraph — faster and more energy-efficient than Ethereum or Bitcoin, with transaction fees under $0.001. Designed for enterprise and government-grade applications.

🏦
Where does my investment money go?

Your funds go into an escrow account held by a licensed partner bank in Rwanda — Imali never holds your money. Funds are only released to the project when verified milestones are reached.

📊
How do I know a project will succeed?

Every project must score 75+ on the Imali Readiness Assessment, with mandatory 100% on Legal and Tokenisation categories. We only list projects that pass — so you never see risky, unvetted deals.

📱
Do I need to understand crypto?

No. Imali manages the technical blockchain layer for you. Your wallet is created automatically when you verify your account. You interact with it through this dashboard — no wallets, seed phrases, or exchanges required.

Milestone-based releases

Returns are not paid at the end of a project — they are released at each verified milestone (foundation, structure, fit-out, handover). You earn as the project progresses.

02
Token price appreciation

As a project moves through milestones and land value increases, the underlying value of your token increases. Early investors benefit from the greatest appreciation.

03

When a milestone is verified, the smart contract automatically calculates each token holder's share and transfers returns directly to their wallets — without delays or human approval.

04
Landowner equity option

Landowners can choose to receive tokens instead of cash — becoming co-investors in their own land's development. Their tokens gain value as the project succeeds.
